## Employment Support Policies for College Graduates in Shanghai
### (I) Special Talent Subsidy Policies
#### Subsidies Available for Individual Graduates
1.  **One-off Job Hunting and Entrepreneurship Subsidy**: 7 categories of full-time graduates in financial difficulty in the graduation academic year from general higher education institutions in Shanghai who have the willingness to find employment or start a business (including graduates from families receiving minimum living security for urban and rural residents, graduates with disabilities, graduates among persons in extreme poverty, graduates from poverty-alleviated families, graduates who received national student loans during their studies, etc.), can apply for a one-off job hunting and entrepreneurship subsidy. The subsidy standard is **1,000 yuan per person, limited to one time per person**.
2.  **One-off First-time Entrepreneurship Subsidy**: For college graduates with Shanghai household registration who are within 2 years of graduation, who have established a small and micro enterprise, individual industrial and commercial household, farmer cooperative, private non-enterprise unit or other entrepreneurial organization for the first time in Shanghai for more than 1 year, and have paid urban employee social insurance premiums for at least 1 person for more than 6 months in accordance with regulations, can apply for a **one-off first-time entrepreneurship subsidy of 8,000 yuan**.
3.  **Vocational Skills Improvement Subsidy**: Fresh college graduates who obtain intermediate or above vocational skill level certificates in the graduation academic year can enjoy the vocational skills improvement subsidy in accordance with regulations.
4.  **Employment Internship Subsidy**: College graduates can participate in employment and entrepreneurship internships for a maximum of 12 months at recognized internship bases in Shanghai, and enjoy the internship subsidy policy in accordance with regulations.

#### Employment Subsidies for Employing Units
1.  **One-off Job Expansion Subsidy**: From January 1 to December 31, 2025, enterprises and social organizations in Shanghai that recruit 2025 graduates and unemployed college graduates within 2 years of graduation, sign labor contracts, and pay unemployment, work-related injury, and employee endowment insurance premiums in full for more than 3 months in accordance with regulations, can enjoy a one-off job expansion subsidy at the standard of **1,500 yuan per person recruited**. Eligible units will receive the subsidy through a "application-free" method.
2.  **Tax and Fee Reduction for Employment Absorption**: For enterprises that recruit eligible college graduates, a tax and fee reduction of **7,800 yuan per person per year** will be granted within 3 years according to the actual number of recruited personnel.

### (II) Employment and Entrepreneurship Support Policies
1.  **Grassroots Employment Position Support**: In 2025, Shanghai will provide more than 1,000 grassroots positions including "Selected Graduates", "Three Supports and One Assistance Program", and "Western Program". All districts are encouraged to increase the talent reserve of outstanding college graduates, increase the recruitment of primary and secondary school teachers, and continue to implement the special recruitment of community workers.
2.  **Settling Preferential Support**: Enterprises that recruit a large number of Shanghai college graduates will be given priority consideration in the application for key supporting employers for the settlement of non-Shanghai fresh graduates. College graduates who are employed in key areas of Shanghai and meet relevant conditions can enjoy direct settlement, shortened years for household transfer through residence permit, special points for residence permit and other policies in accordance with regulations.
3.  **Tax and Fee Reduction for Entrepreneurship and Operation**: Eligible college graduates in the graduation year who engage in individual business operations can enjoy a sequential tax deduction with a limit of **24,000 yuan per household per year** within 3 years starting from the month of completing the individual industrial and commercial household registration.
4.  **Entrepreneurship Financing Support**: Relax the application conditions for entrepreneurship guaranteed loans for college graduates, allowing them to apply for personal entrepreneurship guaranteed loans without paying off other loans such as student loans and consumer loans. For newly funded enterprises under the "Eagle Program" of the Shanghai Science and Technology Entrepreneurship Foundation for College Students, the first repayment for 2026 Shanghai college graduates will be postponed for 6 months, and the project review, approval and funding links will be appropriately inclined to 2026 Shanghai college graduates.
5.  **Entrepreneurship Carrier Support**: Municipal and district-level entrepreneurship incubation bases in Shanghai are encouraged to provide free business premises and supporting services for college graduates for a certain period of time. When recruiting students for entrepreneurship training such as the "Entrepreneurship Training Camp" and the "Postgraduate Innovation and Entrepreneurship Ability Training Program", priority will be given to 2026 Shanghai college graduates.
6.  **Employability Improvement Support**: Special activities such as "Job Hunting Ability Training Camp", "Malanhua Plan", "Seagull Plan" and "Hongzhi Navigation Plan" will be carried out for college graduates to accurately improve the employability of different groups. Graduates from poverty-alleviated families, low-income families and disabled graduates will be given priority in job recommendation and training. Deepen government-enterprise-school cooperation and jointly build a number of college student employment internship practice bases.

### (III) Social Insurance Subsidy Policies
1.  **Social Insurance Subsidy for Flexible Employment of Unemployed College Graduates After Graduation**: Unemployed college graduates within 2 years of graduation (including college graduates with local household registration and non-local graduates from Shanghai universities) whose first employment is flexible employment in Shanghai and participate in Shanghai's urban employee basic endowment and medical insurance can apply for social insurance subsidies.
2.  The subsidy standard is: 50% of the social insurance premiums calculated based on the lower limit of the employee social insurance payment base in the month of payment. The subsidy period is the actual number of months the insured participates in Shanghai's urban employee basic endowment and medical insurance, with a cumulative maximum of 24 months.
